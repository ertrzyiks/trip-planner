## Why this place matters

Carentan sits at the base of the Cotentin Peninsula where the Douve and Taute rivers converge, controlling the only road corridor between Utah and Omaha beaches. After D-Day, these two American sectors were separated by several miles of flooded marshland with no overland connection. Until Carentan fell, the Utah lodgment and the Omaha lodgment were effectively two isolated beachheads that could not support each other. Closing this gap was the 101st Airborne Division's most critical post-D-Day objective.

The battle lasted from 10 to 12 June 1944 and included some of the most difficult urban and approach fighting the 101st Airborne encountered in Normandy. The assault down the causeway approach roads — four straight-line causeways across open flooded ground, each offering no cover — became known as "Purple Heart Lane." German paratroopers of the 6th Fallschirmjäger Regiment defended with skill, and a counterattack on the morning of 13 June nearly recaptured the town before being broken by artillery and fighter-bomber support.

Carentan's fall on 12 June finally joined the two American beachheads into a single continuous front, a milestone that secured the entire US lodgment in Normandy.

## Key WW2 events and armies

- 1944-06-06 to 1944-06-09: 101st Airborne Division (VIII Corps, US First Army) secures surrounding villages and approaches; German 6th Fallschirmjäger Regiment (II Parachute Corps) defends Carentan and its approaches.
- 1944-06-10 to 1944-06-11: 502nd and 506th Parachute Infantry Regiments advance down the four approach causeways under fire; the attack stalls repeatedly on the exposed routes; the assault on the causeways becomes known as "Purple Heart Lane."
- 1944-06-11 night to 1944-06-12: 506th PIR (including Easy Company, 2nd Battalion) bypasses the causeways through flooded fields in the dark and enters Carentan at dawn; fierce house-to-house fighting clears the town.
- 1944-06-12 midday: Carentan is secured by the 101st; the gap between Utah and Omaha beachheads is closed; a continuous US front now runs from the Cotentin coast to the British sector.
- 1944-06-13 morning: 17th SS Panzergrenadier Division "Götz von Berlichingen" counterattacks from the south and nearly retakes Carentan; broken by the timely arrival of the US 2nd Armored Division and concentrated artillery.

## Important people

- Major General Maxwell Taylor: Commander 101st Airborne Division; directed the Carentan operation and the subsequent defense against the SS counterattack.
- Lieutenant Colonel Robert Cole: Commander 3rd Battalion, 502nd PIR; led the only bayonet charge by US paratroopers in Normandy on the causeway approaches on 11 June; awarded the Medal of Honor for the action; killed in the Netherlands in September 1944.
- Major General J. Lawton Collins: Commander VII Corps; coordinated the link-up between the Utah and Omaha sectors through Carentan.
- SS-Obersturmbannführer Friedrich-August Freiherr von der Heydte: Commander German 6th Fallschirmjäger Regiment; conducted the stubborn defense of Carentan and the approaches before ordering withdrawal on the night of 11–12 June.

## What to see nearby

- Carentan market square and church: The center of the June 12 urban fighting; the church steeple was a key German observation post.
- Dead Man's Corner Museum (Saint-Côme-du-Mont, 5 km north): Covers 101st Airborne operations south of Carentan; named for a German officer's body found at the crossroads; includes a Douglas C-47 mounted outside.
- Utah Beach (20 km north): The beachhead that Carentan's capture finally connected to Omaha.
- Douve River causeways (north of town on the N13): The approach routes of the 502nd and 506th PIR; the straight roads across flooded ground make "Purple Heart Lane" immediately comprehensible.
- La Fière Bridge (25 km north): The parallel 82nd Airborne causeway battle on the Merderet, fought at the same time as Carentan.

## Museums

- Dead Man's Corner Museum (Saint-Côme-du-Mont): The primary museum for 101st Airborne operations in the Carentan sector; artifacts, maps, and personal accounts from the paratroopers who fought here. Allow 1 hour.
- Carentan town hall area: Small memorial and plaques in the town center mark the liberation and the role of Easy Company, 2nd Battalion 506th PIR — made famous by Stephen Ambrose's book and the HBO series *Band of Brothers*.

## Visitor notes

Carentan is a working Norman town; the best WW2 experience comes from combining the Dead Man's Corner Museum (5 km north) with a drive down the D913 causeway approach road into town — the terrain that earned its wartime name is immediately visible. Allow a half-day including the museum. Fans of *Band of Brothers* will recognize the town square and approach routes; the series was filmed partly on location here.
