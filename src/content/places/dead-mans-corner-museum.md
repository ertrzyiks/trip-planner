## Why this place matters

The museum sits at the crossroads where the N13 (the main Cherbourg–Carentan highway) meets the road into Saint-Côme-du-Mont, about 5 km north of Carentan. This junction was a key German strongpoint covering the northern approach to Carentan, and its capture was a necessary step before the 101st Airborne Division could attempt the causeway assault on Carentan itself.

The building housing the museum served as a German command post during the fight for the crossroads on 7 June 1944. After American troops took the position, a German officer's body remained slumped over a disabled tank at the intersection for several days. US troops began referring to the junction as "Dead Man's Corner" in radio traffic and after-action reports, and the name stuck — it appears on period military maps and remains the official name of the crossroads today.

Because the crossroads sat directly on the route the 101st Airborne used to mass for the Carentan causeway attack, the museum's location ties together the airborne landings of 6 June, the fight for Saint-Côme-du-Mont, and the opening of "Purple Heart Lane" toward Carentan on 10–12 June.

## Key WW2 events and armies

- 1944-06-06 to 1944-06-07: Elements of the 101st Airborne Division (501st and 506th Parachute Infantry Regiments) assemble south and east of Saint-Côme-du-Mont after a scattered night drop; German 6th Fallschirmjäger Regiment (II Parachute Corps) holds the village and the N13 crossroads.
- 1944-06-07: Fighting at the N13/Saint-Côme-du-Mont crossroads; a German officer's body is left on a disabled armored vehicle at the junction, giving rise to the "Dead Man's Corner" name used by US forces for the rest of the campaign.
- 1944-06-08: Saint-Côme-du-Mont falls to the 101st Airborne, securing the northern approach to Carentan and opening the staging ground for the causeway assault.
- 1944-06-10 to 1944-06-12: 502nd and 506th PIR stage through this area for the "Purple Heart Lane" advance down the flooded causeways into Carentan; exact unit boundaries through the crossroads vary by source.

## Important people

- Colonel Robert Sink: Commander 506th Parachute Infantry Regiment; his regiment fought through Saint-Côme-du-Mont and the surrounding crossroads in the days after the drop.
- Lieutenant Colonel Robert Cole: Commander 3rd Battalion, 502nd PIR; operated through this sector during the advance toward Carentan before leading the Medal of Honor bayonet charge on the causeway on 11 June.
- Oberstleutnant Friedrich-August Freiherr von der Heydte: Commander German 6th Fallschirmjäger Regiment; ordered the defense of Saint-Côme-du-Mont and the N13 crossroads before withdrawing toward Carentan.

## What to see nearby

- Saint-Côme-du-Mont church and village (1 km): The objective the 101st Airborne fought to secure on 7–8 June; the village sits at the edge of the original drop zone area.
- Angoville-au-Plain (4 km east): An 11th-century church where two 101st Airborne medics treated wounded from both sides; the wooden pews still show bloodstains.
- Carentan (5 km south): The town the crossroads fight opened the route toward; combine for a half-day 101st Airborne itinerary.
- Purple Heart Lane / D913 causeway (south of the crossroads): The flooded approach roads the 502nd and 506th PIR assaulted on 10–12 June.
- Sainte-Mère-Église (10 km north): The Airborne Museum and the wider 82nd/101st Airborne story for the Cotentin.

## Museums

- Dead Man's Corner Museum: Housed in the former German command post building at the crossroads; displays uniforms, weapons, vehicles, and personal items of the 101st Airborne paratroopers who fought through this sector, with a Douglas C-47 Skytrain mounted outside. Allow about 1 hour.

## Visitor notes

The museum sits directly on the N13, making it an easy stop between Sainte-Mère-Église and Carentan. Allow about 1 hour for the exhibits. Combine with a short detour to Angoville-au-Plain (its medic chapel is one of the most moving small sites in Normandy) and a drive down the D913 causeway into Carentan to connect the crossroads fight to "Purple Heart Lane."
