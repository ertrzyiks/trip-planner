# WW2 Place Content Guide

This folder stores WW2 travel content for Normandy and related campaign locations.

## Purpose
Write each place page for visitors passionate about WW2 military history.
Each page must help a traveler understand what happened there, who was involved, and what to visit next.

## Required File Format
Use Markdown with this exact frontmatter shape:

```md
---
name: "<Place Name>"
description: "<One-sentence historical summary>"
captureDate: "YYYY-MM-DD"
---
```

Then use the following required sections in this order:

1. `## Why this place matters`
2. `## Key WW2 events and armies`
3. `## Important people`
4. `## What to see nearby`
5. `## Museums`
6. `## Read more online`
7. `## Visitor notes`

## Section Rules

### 1) Why this place matters
- 2 to 4 short paragraphs.
- Explain the military significance in the campaign timeline.
- Mention operation names (for example: Operation Overlord, Operation Neptune, Battle of Cherbourg).

### 2) Key WW2 events and armies
- Use bullet points with date, event, and forces involved.
- Include both Allied and German formations when known.
- If uncertain, say `historians disagree` or `exact unit attribution varies by source`.
- Example bullet style:
  - `1944-06-22 to 1944-06-26: US VII Corps (US First Army) fought German garrison units in urban and port defenses.`

### 3) Important people
- Use bullet points.
- Include role and why they matter at this place.
- Prefer commanders, local resistance figures, or known war correspondents tied directly to the location.

### 4) What to see nearby
- Use bullet points.
- Include viewpoints, memorials, cemeteries, batteries, beaches, bridges, and old town areas.
- Keep items reachable as day-trip or short detour suggestions.

### 5) Museums
- Use bullet points.
- Include museum name and one line on what is unique there.
- If known, include practical hint (`closed on Tuesdays`, `book ahead`, etc.).

### 6) Read more online
- Use bullet points in this style:
  - `[Title](https://example.com) - 1 sentence on what the reader gets.`
- Prefer trustworthy sources: museum sites, official memorials, national archives, reputable military history organizations.
- Avoid low-trust SEO listicles.

### 7) Visitor notes
- Short practical advice for a history-focused visit.
- Mention ideal visit duration and how to combine with nearby sites.

## Writing Style
- Tone: informed, factual, travel-friendly.
- Prefer concrete details over vague phrases.
- Keep each file concise but useful.
- Use neutral language for all armies and nations.

## Quality Checklist
Before finalizing a place file, verify:

- Frontmatter fields (`name`, `description`, `captureDate`) are present.
- All required sections exist in the required order.
- Events include dates and military formations.
- At least 3 important people are listed when possible.
- At least 3 nearby spots and 1 museum are listed when possible.
- At least 3 online reading links are included.
- No unsupported claims presented as certain facts.
